Composer's Final Exercise

Choose one of your own compositions.

Without changing a single note,

rewrite the orchestration from beginning to end.

Ask yourself at every measure:

Can the emotion be stronger?
Can the texture be clearer?
Can the balance improve?
Can fewer instruments achieve more?
Can one instrument tell the story better?

Then compare both versions.

Notice that orchestration alone can completely transform a piece of music.

Professional Orchestrator Checklist

Before completing any orchestration ask:

✓ Does every instrument have a purpose?

✓ Is the melody always clear?

✓ Is the emotional journey obvious?

✓ Does the orchestra breathe naturally?

✓ Are dynamics shaping the story?

✓ Is the climax earned?

✓ Is silence used intentionally?

✓ Would removing something improve the music?

✓ Does the orchestration support the narrative?

✓ Would the audience remember the emotion more than the technique?
