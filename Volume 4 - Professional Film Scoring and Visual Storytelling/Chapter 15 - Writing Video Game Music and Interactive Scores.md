Chapter 15 — Writing Video Game Music and Interactive Scores

"Unlike film music, game music is not written for one fixed story. It is written for thousands of possible stories the player may create."

Introduction

Video game music presents challenges unlike any other form of composition.

A film always unfolds the same way.

A game rarely does.

The player decides:

Where to go.
How long to stay.
Whether to fight.
Whether to explore.
Whether to succeed or fail.

Professional game composers therefore write music that can adapt without losing its identity.

The score must remain emotionally convincing regardless of the player's actions.

Examples

Exploration

↓

↓

Modal mixture.

Combat

Victory

Harmony supports gameplay without restarting the cue.

Looping

Unlike film,

game music often loops.

Professional loops should feel continuous.

Avoid obvious beginnings and endings.

A listener should not immediately recognize where the loop restarts.

Exploration Music

Exploration rewards curiosity.

Characteristics include:

Slow harmonic rhythm.

Floating melodies.

Transparent orchestration.

Wide textures.

Gentle dynamics.

The player should enjoy remaining in the environment.

Combat Music

Combat requires:

Momentum.

Urgency.

Rhythmic drive.

Clear pulse.

Controlled intensity.

Avoid exhausting the player.

Energy should rise and fall naturally.

Boss Themes

Boss battles deserve unique musical identities.

A boss theme should communicate:

Power.

Danger.

Importance.

Personality.

The audience should immediately recognize that this encounter is different.

Maintaining Identity

Regardless of gameplay,

the soundtrack should feel unified.

Recurring themes,

harmonic language,

instrumentation,

and orchestration create continuity throughout the game.

Professional Game Music Checklist

Before completing a cue ask:

✓ Can the music loop naturally?

✓ Can it adapt to gameplay?

✓ Does every location have an identity?

✓ Are transitions seamless?

✓ Is player feedback supported?

✓ Does the soundtrack remain unified?

✓ Will players remember the themes after finishing the game?

Chapter Summary

Professional game scoring means:

Writing adaptive music.
Designing seamless loops.
Creating memorable environmental themes.
Supporting gameplay through musical feedback.
Building unified musical worlds.
Allowing music to evolve with the player's experience.

The greatest game scores do not simply accompany gameplay.

They become part of the player's memories of the adventure itself.

"Players may choose their own path, but a great game score ensures that every path feels like part of the same unforgettable journey."
