Chapter 17 — Horizontal Re-Sequencing and Non-Linear Music

"In interactive media, music does not always move from beginning to end. Sometimes it chooses its own path."

Introduction

Vertical layering allows music to grow by adding or removing layers.

Horizontal re-sequencing solves a different problem.

Instead of changing layers,

it changes which musical section plays next.

This allows music to adapt to gameplay while preserving complete musical phrases.

Professional composers use horizontal re-sequencing extensively in:

Video Games
Interactive Installations
VR Experiences
Museum Exhibits
Adaptive Storytelling

The listener experiences one continuous musical journey, even though the order of sections changes dynamically.

Harmonic Compatibility

Changing modules should never create harmonic confusion.

Common techniques include:

Shared tonic
Pedal tones
Pivot chords
Modal consistency
Sustained drones

Harmony should support flexibility.

Tempo Consistency

Most adaptive modules remain at one tempo.

This allows seamless transitions.

If tempo changes,

professional systems often:

Fade gradually
Use transition cues
Insert bridge modules

Abrupt tempo changes rarely sound natural.
