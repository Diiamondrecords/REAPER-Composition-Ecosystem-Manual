Chapter 24 — Writing Trailer Music and Promotional Scores

"A trailer score has one purpose: convince the audience that they must experience the story."

Introduction

Trailer music is one of the most recognizable forms of modern cinematic composition.

Unlike a film score,

a trailer does not tell the complete story.

It creates anticipation.

Curiosity.

Excitement.

Wonder.

The audience should finish the trailer wanting more.

Professional trailer composers write music that builds emotional momentum from beginning to end.

The Purpose of Trailer Music

A trailer score must:

Capture attention immediately.
Build continuous excitement.
Support visual editing.
Highlight dramatic moments.
End memorably.

Unlike traditional film scoring,

the trailer often becomes the emotional engine driving the entire advertisement.

Three-Act Trailer Structure

Many successful trailers follow a three-part musical structure.

Act One

Introduction.

Curiosity.

Atmosphere.

Minimal orchestration.

Act Two

Development.

Growing tension.

Rhythmic movement.

Themes begin appearing.

Act Three

Full cinematic climax.

Maximum orchestra.

Heavy percussion.

Choir.

Large emotional release.

Hook The Listener

The first ten seconds are critical.

Professional techniques include:

Unique sound design.
Solo instrument.
Memorable motif.
Powerful drone.
Unexpected harmony.

The audience should immediately become curious.

Building Momentum

Trailer music rarely stays static.

Energy constantly increases through:

More instruments.

Braams

Modern trailers often use:

Large brass impacts.

Heavy hybrid hits.

Low orchestral stabs.

Deep synth layers.

These powerful accents are often called braams.

Professionals use them sparingly to emphasize important visual moments.

Risers

↓

Pitch bends.

Stops

Massive build.

Composer's Exercise

Write a two-minute trailer cue.

Structure it into:

Act One

Atmosphere.

Act Two

Build.

Act Three

Epic climax.

Include:

One hero motif.
Three impact moments.
Two risers.
One dramatic silence.
One final orchestral climax.
Professional Trailer Checklist

Before completing a trailer cue ask:

✓ Does the opening capture attention?

✓ Does energy continually increase?

✓ Are impacts meaningful?

✓ Are pauses used effectively?

✓ Does orchestration reach an earned climax?

✓ Can editors easily cut to the music?

✓ Will audiences remember the final moment?

Chapter Summary

Professional trailer scoring means:

Building continuous momentum.
Organizing music into three dramatic acts.
Combining orchestra and modern production.
Using impacts, risers, and silence strategically.
Supporting visual editing.
Creating anticipation rather than resolution.

The best trailer music does not reveal the story.

It makes the audience eager to discover it.

"A great trailer score ends before the audience is satisfied, leaving them with only one desire—to experience the complete story."
